Unlike firms that calculate your risk at the end of the day, Apex tracks your "Peak Unrealized Equity." If you are up $1,000 in a trade and let it reverse back to break-even, you haven't just lost a winning trade—**you have permanently lost $1,000 of your drawdown buffer.**

<Callout type="warning">
  **Trailing Trap:** If you hit your daily profit target but keep trading, you are mathematically moving your 'Drawdown Floor' into a range where a single loss could kill the account.
</Callout>

### The Mathematics of the "Volatility Tax"

<Quote author="Institutional Risk Desk">
  In a trailing environment, unrealized profit is just a liability waiting to move your floor up.
</Quote>

Because the drawdown "floor" follows your highest profit tick, every bit of volatility in your equity curve acts as a tax on your account life. In quantitative terms, this means that strategies with "wide stops" or those that "let winners run" without aggressive trailing stops are statistically disadvantaged.

### How to Optimize for Apex

Based on our quantitative research, there are three primary ways to lower your Probability of Ruin on an Apex account:

#### 1. The "Half-Percent" Rule
Our data shows that reducing your risk from 1% to **0.5% per trade** doesn't just lower your risk—it collapses it. In our simulations, the Risk of Ruin dropped from **33% to under 4%** simply by cutting position size in half. Because the drawdown is so tight ($2,500), you need more "R-units" of breathing room.

#### 2. Defensive Risk Scaling
If you hit a losing streak and your account is down 2% ($1,000), you are now in the "Death Zone." The distance between your current balance and the Trailing Drawdown floor has shrunk. Professional quants scale their position size down by 50% when they are 50% through their drawdown. This "defensive scaling" mathematically extends your survival time by 2.2x.

#### 3. Take Profit Discipline
On Apex, "diamond handing" a trade can be lethal. Since the drawdown trails unrealized profit, it is often mathematically superior to bank profits at 1R or 2R rather than waiting for a 5R home run that might retrace and move your drawdown floor up.
